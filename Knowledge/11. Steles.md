There comes a time in every magus' (usually short) lifetime when they need to "quick cast" something. Ideally, this usually needs to come from a source _besides_ their arcana pools. This function is something that steles specialize in.

Steles are magical "runestones" ritually crafted with a single spell inside it. They are stuffed with arcana during their creation, which the held spell makes use of when the stele is used.

During the crafting process, the ritual space may become unstable and open Arcane Tears. These gradually become larger and larger until they shatter, causing some effect to take place in most cases. They can be closed by using a Staff (while the offhand does not contain any spell castable items). Closing them is rewarding, as they add more arcana to the overall stele ritual when they are closed. Additionally, the initiator of the ritual can check the amount of arcana stored inside the ritual by using a Staff on the Lectern used as the base of the ritual's power.

Historically, steles were tools of lazy magick, a term used for magical techniques believed to take the "magic" out of the Akashic Arts. However, their ceremonial (and sometimes, communal) creation method has allowed them to be tolerated and even celebrated. There are now many magi that make a profit by selling steles to ignorant Mundanes and allied Witches, or provide them to their plagiarist peers in an attempt to keep their magical methods secret.
