Spell Books, Ancient Lores and Scribe Notes may all be placed into Chiseled Bookshelves. However, Spell Pages do not have this same luxury. Why? The answer is simple. Because they are placed inside somewhere else where they are much more useful: the Scroll Shelf.

It is crafted by chiseling a Chiseled Bookshelf. They are useful because spell pages placed inside them are saved to the world. By using Archive Incants, a Magus (or some other source...) may pull an inserted spell page's incantation simply by knowing the location of the Scroll Shelf and the specific section the spell is in. Naturally, there are privacy risks to this.

Regardless, there have been many mage libraries over the millennia. Some keep their dangerous spells on their shelves, trapped with dangerous Thoth-born baneful magicks. Others keep their most prized spells bundled in with useless rabble. The most brilliant do not hide their secrets, instead allowing curiosity to run its course, effectively killing the prying cats with not a smudge of satisfaction to resurrect them.
