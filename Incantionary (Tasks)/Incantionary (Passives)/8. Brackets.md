Arcanic has its own way of representing ( and ), partly because they are important for the creation of List Clusters. What these Incants are listed below:

**ABI & VIR**

ABI and VIR are dualistic incants that you will not see without each other. They mean "beginning" and "ending" respectively, and both meanings are characteristics that describe their use.

        ABI opens the to-be List Cluster. Every word and incant after this ABI is thrown into the List that has "begun" to take form.

        VIR, on the other hand, closes and finalizes the List Cluster, making it complete! The incants inside will not be cast, so long as they are not triggered by an incant that provides that function (eg. Logics, and certain Akashicals).

        Together, ABI VIR creates List Clusters, a cluster type that is the key to higher end Akashic Magic. The List Cluster created is naturally added to the top of the Akashic Stack.

**Example Spells:**

ABI VIR DEBUG -> Allows the caster to see the Akashic and view an empty List Cluster.

ABI ETE GEA VIR ETE GEA DEBUG -> Allows the caster to see the Akashic and view a List Cluster with the contents ETE and GEA and then a Vector Cluster. Note how the contents in the list are not performed, almost as though separate from the rest of the spell.
